  • Teaching Philosophy

As a teacher, I believe education is not just about imparting knowledge but about inspiring curiosity, creativity, and confidence. My philosophy is grounded in the belief that every student has the capacity to learn, create, and trhrive when provided with meaningful, hands-on experiences and supportive relationships.

In TAS, I aim to nurture the innate creativity of my students by encouraging them to explore, design, and innovate. Through authentic, project-based learning, I want my classroom to be a space where ideas come to life - whether that is in the workshops, kitchens, or labs.

In Mathematics, I Seek to help students see the beauty and logic behind numbers. I encourage them to approach problems as opportunities for discovery rather than obstacles. By connecting abstract concepts to real-world applications, I hope to show that mathematics is not only relevant but empowering.

As a VET-trained Hospitality Teacher, I value practical learning and industry relevance. Teaching students skills that translate beyound the classroom fosters both confidence and employability.

Ultimately, I see my role as a facilitator of lifelong learning-helping students become creative thinkers, critical problem-solvers, and compassionate individuals ready to contribute meaningfully to their communities.

"Education is not the learning of facts, but the training of the mind to think."

- Albert Einstein
